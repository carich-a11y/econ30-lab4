# Knowledge Base Index

## Core Mechanisms

- [[motherhood-penalty-mechanisms]] — Cross-source synthesis of the main pathways that generate motherhood penalties over time.
- [[status-based-discrimination]] — Employer bias that marks mothers as less competent or committed than otherwise identical workers.
- [[child-penalties]] — The persistent earnings losses women experience after first birth through employment, hours, and wage channels.
- [[gender-norms-and-child-penalties]] — How culture and place shape the size of child penalties across states, countries, and groups.
- [[parental-leave-and-job-continuity]] — How leave policy can protect employment ties after birth without fully erasing long-run penalties.
- [[greedy-work]] — Jobs that reward long or inflexible hours disproportionately, making caregiving especially costly.

## Work And Family

- [[face-time-culture]] — Workplace norms that equate commitment with long hours, office presence, and constant availability.
- [[schedule-control]] — The ability to control when and where work happens, a key mechanism linking caregiving to career continuity.
- [[nonlinear-career-paths]] — A life-cycle model of careers with pauses, plateaus, and later peaks instead of a single uninterrupted climb.
- [[motherhood-and-scientific-careers]] — How children affect productivity timing, tenure, and survival in scientific careers.

## Historical Context

- [[historical-normalization-of-married-womens-work]] — The shift from explicit barriers to married women's employment toward more subtle modern penalties.

## Economic History
